Emacs and HTTP(s) proxies

If you are using emacs and HTTP related functionalities, you could use the following snippet to reach a domain through an HTTP proxy. It works for most of the stuff I've tested so far, including the handy rest-client and package-*.

(defun unset-proxy
    (&args)
  (interactive "p")
  (setenv "http_proxy" nil)
  (setenv "https_proxy" nil)
  (setq url-proxy-services nil))


(defun set-proxy
    (&args)
  (interactive "p")
  (setq url-proxy-services
    '(("no_proxy" . "^\\(localhost\\|127.*\\)")
      ("http" . "proxy.yourdomain.com:8080")
      ("https" . "proxy.yourdomain.com:8080"))))

Just after evaluation, you can use M-x set-proxy and M-x unset-proxy.
